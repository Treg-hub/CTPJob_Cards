The Problem It Solves

In a traditional plant environment, fault reporting and maintenance response suffer from well-known, costly problems:

ProblemBusiness Impact
No formal fault record — verbal and paper reports are lost or incompleteNo history, no accountability, no basis for analysis
No accountability — unknown when faults were reported, attended to, or resolvedDisputes, delays, inability to measure performance

No pattern recognition — no structured data to identify recurring failuresRepeat breakdowns, reactive-only maintenance culture

The CTP Job Cards platform addresses all of these by digitising the entire process — from fault report to closure — with automated notifications, escalation, and a permanent audit trail.

How It Works

The Process — End to End

1
Fault Reported by Operator

An operator discovers a fault and creates a job card on their phone — recording the machine, area, component, fault description, and priority. The job is live in the system the moment it is submitted.

2
Relevant Technicians Notified Instantly

The system identifies which technicians are on site (via real-time geofencing) and their trade (mechanical, electrical, or both). They receive a push notification on their phone within seconds — no dispatcher, no radio call.

3
Technician Accepts the Job

The technician assigns themselves directly from the notification. The operator is notified immediately. Every response — or non-response — is logged with a timestamp.

4
Automatic Escalation if No Response

If no technician responds, the system escalates automatically — first to foremen and on-site managers, then to department managers and the workshop manager. No critical fault can be silently ignored.

5
Job Tracked to Closure

The technician updates the job status and closes it with a completion note. The operator is notified. The full record — fault to fix — is stored permanently and is searchable.

6
Management Oversight

Managers have a live dashboard of all department jobs, technician on-site status, escalation logs, and notification history. They are responsible for enforcing data quality standards.

Road Ahead

AI & Predictive Maintenance

The current platform is a foundation. Its long-term value lies in what the structured data it collects makes possible.
Phase 1

Predictive Maintenance Analytics

Machine learning applied to job card history identifies failure patterns, predicts which assets are likely to fail, and triggers proactive maintenance before breakdowns occur. Shifts the operation from reactive to predictive.

Phase 2

AI Assistant — Manuals & Fault Finding

A conversational assistant integrated into the app gives technicians instant access to operator manuals, historical repair records, wiring diagrams, and parts specifications — in plain language, on their phone.

Example AI Assistant Queries

What are the most common causes of overload faults on Conveyor 3?
What was done the last three times Machine 7 had a similar fault?
What is the correct bearing specification for the FX400 gearbox drive shaft?
