Onboarding & App Permissions

Who Uses This App

There are four types of users in the CTP Job Cards system:

Operator

Reports faults and creates job cards when a machine or process has an issue.

Technician

Receives job notifications, attends to faults, and closes jobs when resolved.

Manager

Oversees job cards in their department and ensures quality of records.

Admin

Manages system, employee accounts, and configuration.

ℹ️

Operators and Technicians are separate roles. An operator is the person who discovers and reports the fault. A technician is the person who goes to fix it.

Logging In

You log in using your Google email address and password. Your account is linked to your clock number, which determines your role, your department, and what you see in the app.

If you have forgotten your password, tap "Forgot Password" on the login screen and a reset link will be sent to your email.

First-Time Setup: App Permissions

When you open the app for the first time, it will ask for several permissions. Each one is required for the app to work correctly. Do not skip or deny these.

1
Location — "Allow All the Time"
App asks: "Allow CTP Job Cards to access your location all the time."

The app uses your location to determine whether you are on site or off site. Only on-site technicians receive job card notifications. When you enter or leave the company boundary, the system logs it automatically and your on-site status updates in real time.

Location is not used for surveillance. It is used solely to determine whether you are within the company boundary.

✓ Select "Allow All the Time" — choosing "Only While Using the App" will break background geofencing.
2
Notifications — "Allow"
App asks: "Allow CTP Job Cards to send you notifications."

Every job card alert, escalation, and urgent fault reaches you through notifications. Without this permission you will receive nothing — the system will escalate jobs as if no one is responding.

✓ Always select "Allow."
3
Battery Optimisation — "Unrestricted"
App asks: "Allow CTP Job Cards to run in the background without restrictions."

Android phones aggressively shut down background apps to save battery. If the app is "optimised," your phone may kill it after a few minutes, causing you to miss notifications entirely — especially overnight.

✓ Go to Settings → Apps → CTP Job Cards → Battery → Unrestricted.
4
Display Over Other Apps
App asks: "Allow CTP Job Cards to appear over other apps."

Priority 5 faults — production is standing — require a full-screen alert that appears even if your phone is locked or another app is open. This permission makes that possible.

✓ Grant this permission. Without it, P5 alerts fall back to a standard banner, which is easily missed.
5
Do Not Disturb Access
App asks: "Allow CTP Job Cards to override Do Not Disturb."

If your phone is in Do Not Disturb mode, standard notifications are silenced. This permission allows P5 critical alarms to bypass DND so the alarm still sounds when production is standing.

✓ Grant this permission for full P5 coverage.
6
Schedule Exact Alarms
App asks: "Allow CTP Job Cards to set exact alarms."

The P5 full-screen alarm uses the phone's alarm system to guarantee it fires at exactly the right moment, even in deep sleep mode. Without this, the alarm may arrive late or not at all.

✓ Grant when prompted. On some phones: Settings → Apps → Special App Access → Alarms & Reminders.

Flow of Job Cards

What is a Job Card?

A job card is a formal digital record of a fault, breakdown, or maintenance task. It captures the department, area, machine, component, fault description, priority, and type (Mechanical / Electrical / Mech-Elec). It is the system's source of truth for every fault on site.

Priority Levels

Priority reflects the real production impact at the time the fault is reported:

PriorityProduction ImpactExpected Response
P1No effect on production — routine or planned workAttend when available
P2Minor impact — production continuingAttend soon
P3Moderate impact — degraded operationAttend within the shift
P4Significant impact — output reducedAttend as soon as possible
P5Production is standing — stoppedImmediate response
⚠️

Priority must be honest. A P5 means production has stopped. Using P5 routinely when production is running erodes the system's ability to distinguish genuine emergencies. Managers monitor priority accuracy.

Job Card Status

A job card moves through four statuses during its life:

StatusMeaning
OpenJob has been created and is awaiting a technician — no one has accepted it yet
In-ProgressA technician has self-assigned and is actively working the job — escalation has stopped
MonitorFault has been resolved but the machine is being watched for recurrence
ClosedJob is complete — fault resolved and confirmed
ℹ️

The moment you tap Assign Self on a notification (or open a job from the alert), the job card automatically moves from Open to In-Progress. You do not need to change it manually.

For Operators: Creating a Job Card

When you discover a fault or breakdown, create a job card immediately. Do not wait.

  1. Open the CTP Job Cards app
  2. Tap Create Job Card
  3. Fill in all fields — department, area, machine, part, description, and priority
  4. Submit — the relevant technicians are notified instantly

What Good Looks Like

Instead of...Write...
"Machine broken""Conveyor 3 drive motor tripping on overload — production line stopped"
"Pump issue""Cooling water pump P-02 not priming — no flow to chiller circuit"
"Fault on line""PLC fault code E-14 on Line 2 pressing machine — cycle will not complete"

Once submitted, you can track the job's status in the app. You will receive a notification when a technician accepts the job and again when it is closed.

For Technicians: Receiving and Working a Job

Step 1 — Receive the Notification

When a job matching your trade is created and you are on site, you receive a push notification showing the job number, priority, machine, area, and description. You have three options directly from the notification:

ActionWhat it does
Assign SelfYou take ownership of the job. The operator is notified. Escalation stops immediately.
I'm BusyYou acknowledge the job but cannot attend right now. The operator is notified. Escalation stops. The system continues looking for another technician.
DismissYou dismiss the alert. This is logged. Escalation continues until someone accepts.
Assign Self I'm Busy Dismiss
🔴

Always respond to notifications. If you do not respond, the system escalates the job to your foreman and then your manager. All responses are visible in the notification log.

Step 2 — Work the Job

Once accepted, navigate to My Assigned Jobs in the app to view full details. Update the status as you progress:

  • When you self-assign the job: the status changes automatically from Open to In-Progress
  • When you have resolved the fault but want to watch the machine: change to Monitor
  • When the job is fully complete: change to Closed

Step 3 — Close the Job

When closing a job you must add a note. A closed job with no note is an incomplete record. Include:

  • What was done — the actual repair or action taken
  • Parts used — including numbers or specifications where possible
  • Root cause — wear, contamination, operator error, age, incorrect adjustment?
  • Recommendations — any follow-up work or scheduled maintenance needed

The operator who raised the job is automatically notified when you close it.

Automatic Escalation: What Happens If Nobody Responds?

The system has automatic escalation across four configurable stages. Each stage has its own timer and recipient list, configured by Admin under Settings → Escalation Rules. The defaults are:

StageDefault Time After CreationDefault Recipients
Stage 12 minutesForemen and on-site managers for the relevant department
Stage 27 minutesDepartment managers and the workshop manager (urgent alert)
Stage 330 minutesSenior management
Stage 460 minutesFinal escalation tier

Escalation stops the moment any technician self-assigns or responds "I'm Busy." If your site uses different timings, your Admin can change them at any time — the defaults above are a starting point, not a fixed rule.

On-Site Arrival & Departure Notifications

When you cross the company boundary you automatically receive:

  • "Arrived On-Site" — your status updates and job notifications begin
  • "Left Site Area" — your status updates and job notifications pause until you return

This is fully automatic — no action required from you.

Section 4 · App Appearance — Light & Dark Mode

The app supports both a dark theme (the default — easier on the eyes in low-light environments such as workshops and night shifts) and a light theme (better visibility in bright outdoor sunlight).

To switch:

  1. Open the app and tap the gear icon (Settings) at the top right
  2. Find the Dark Mode toggle near the top of the Settings screen
  3. Tap the switch to flip between themes — the change is instant

Your choice is remembered and applies every time you open the app. The orange CTP branding remains consistent across both themes.

The Future: AI-Assisted Maintenance

Predictive Maintenance

By analysing the history of job cards — which machines fail most often, at what intervals, under what conditions — the system will generate predictive alerts:

  • "Machine X has had 4 bearing failures in 6 months. Inspection recommended within 3 weeks."
  • "Fault frequency on Line 3 increases in summer. Pre-emptive service advised."

This shifts the team from reacting to breakdowns to planning interventions during scheduled downtime.

AI Chatbot — Manuals & Fault Finding

A conversational AI assistant will be integrated into the app with access to operator manuals, historical fault records, wiring diagrams, and parts lists. A technician on the floor will be able to ask:

"The conveyor on Line 2 is tripping on overload. What are the common causes?"
"What is the torque spec for the main shaft bearing on the FX400 gearbox?"
"What was done the last time this fault appeared on Machine 7?"

The assistant responds instantly with answers drawn from official documentation and real maintenance history.

Your job card entries today become the data that makes these AI tools accurate. Complete descriptions and clear closure notes are an investment in the system's future intelligence.
