This is the authorised installation page for the CTP Gravure internal job card tracking app. It is built and maintained by CTP Gravure and is intended exclusively for CTP Gravure staff — operators, technicians, and managers.

Why isn't this on the Google Play Store?

CTP Job Cards is a private internal tool. Publishing it on the Play Store would make it publicly visible to anyone outside CTP Gravure, which is inappropriate for an internal workflow system. Instead, it is distributed through Firebase App Distribution — Google's official enterprise platform for internal app delivery. This gives CTP full control over who receives the app and which version they run.

All data is stored on Google Firebase, Google's enterprise application platform. It is not stored on a personal server, a laptop, or any device that could be lost or stolen.

South Africa Data Region

Data is stored in the africa-south1 (Johannesburg) cloud region. It does not leave South Africa.

Encrypted at Rest & in Transit

All data is encrypted using AES-256 encryption at rest. All traffic between the app and Firebase uses HTTPS/TLS encryption.

Google's Security Infrastructure

Firebase runs on Google Cloud, which holds ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, and other international security certifications. The same infrastructure used by enterprises worldwide.

Authentication by Firebase Auth

Login is handled by Firebase Authentication — Google's secure identity service. Passwords are hashed and never stored in plain text. Password resets are email-based.

Role-Restricted Access

What you can read or write in the app is enforced by Firestore security rules — your role determines your access level. Operators cannot see technician-only data and vice versa.

No Advertising or Analytics

Firebase does not use your data for advertising. No third-party analytics or tracking SDKs are included in the app. Your data is used exclusively to operate the job card system.

In plain terms: your data sits in a locked Google data centre in Johannesburg. Only Gert Peens has the key to the Firebase Console. Google protects the infrastructure but does not read, sell, or access your data. The app itself only reads and writes what it needs to function as a maintenance management tool — nothing more.

CTP Job Cards replaces the paper-based job card system at CTP Gravure with a real-time digital alternative. When a fault or breakdown occurs, operators create a job card in the app. The relevant on-site technicians are notified immediately on their phones — no dispatcher, no radio, no delay. Managers stay informed at every step through dashboards and escalation alerts.

  • → Full audit trail of every action on every job card
  • → Works offline — syncs automatically when connectivity returns

Permissions the app will request (6 total — all required)

The app guides you through these permissions during first-time setup. Each one enables a specific function. Denying any of them will cause the app to not work correctly.

1 · Location — "Allow All the Time"
Required for background geofencing — the app detects when you arrive or leave the site boundary and adjusts your on-site status automatically. You must select "Allow All the Time"; choosing "Only While Using the App" means geofencing stops the moment you put your phone in your pocket. Only on-site/off-site status is tracked — not your precise location.
2 · Notifications — "Allow"
All job card alerts reach you through push notifications. Without this, you will receive nothing — the system will escalate past you as if no one is available.
3 · Battery Optimisation — "Unrestricted"
Android automatically puts idle apps to sleep to conserve battery. If the app is "optimised," Android may put it to sleep while it is idle — this can cause missed notifications and prevent geofence entry and exit events from being detected. Setting it to Unrestricted keeps the app available to receive alerts at any time. Navigate to Settings → Apps → CTP Job Cards → Battery → Unrestricted.
4 · Display Over Other Apps
Priority 5 faults only. A P5 job card means production is standing — someone must respond immediately. This permission lets the app display a full-screen alarm over your lock screen and any open app so a P5 fault cannot go unnoticed. Without it, P5 alerts fall back to a standard banner that can be easily missed.
5 · Do Not Disturb Access
Allows P5 critical alarms to bypass DND mode — essential for night-shift coverage and for phones that are set to silent. Standard notifications are still silenced by DND; only P5 production-stopping faults override it.
6 · Schedule Exact Alarms
The P5 alarm uses Android's alarm system (the same system as your morning alarm) to guarantee the alert fires at precisely the right time, even if the phone is in deep sleep. Find this under Settings → Apps → Special App Access → Alarms & Reminders.

How to Install Safely

The first step is to register your email so Firebase can verify you as an authorised user. After that, installing the APK takes about 2 minutes.

Only install APK files from this page or from a Firebase email sent to your registered address. If anyone sends you an APK file by WhatsApp, USB drive, or any other method — do not install it. Contact Gert first.

1
Register with Firebase App Distribution

Tap "Register to Download" in the Download section below, or scan the QR code. You will be taken to the Firebase App Distribution invite page — sign in with your personal Gmail to register as an authorised user.

Note: If your Gmail has not been added to the authorised list yet, contact Gert Peens at gertp@ctpgravure.co.za before proceeding.

2
Download the APK from the Firebase page

Once signed in, tap the download button on the Firebase App Distribution page. Your browser will download the APK file to your phone.

3
Open the APK and allow installation from this source

Tap the downloaded file. Android will show a security prompt: "For your security, your phone is not allowed to install unknown apps from this source." This is normal and expected for all internal company apps — it simply means the app was not downloaded from the Play Store.

Android 8+: Tap "Settings" on the dialog → Enable "Allow from this source" → Press back → Tap "Install".

Older Android: Go to Settings → Security → Unknown sources → Enable → Return and install.

4
Complete the installation

Tap Install on the Android installer screen. The app will appear on your home screen once complete.

5
Log in with your email

Open the app and log in using the same email address you registered with. Your role and department are configured by Gert Peens on the backend — you will see only the screens relevant to your role.

6
Complete the permission setup

The app walks you through all 6 permissions on first launch. Grant each one — see the "Permissions" section above for a full explanation of why each is needed.
