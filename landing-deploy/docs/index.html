Start here

If this is your first time

Sign in with your company email. The app walks you through a 7-page onboarding that explains priorities, escalation, and the permissions it needs. After that, the screens you see depend on your role.

  • Operator — you create job cards when something breaks.
  • Technician — you receive alerts and fix faults.
  • Manager — you oversee jobs and respond to escalations.
  • Admin — you configure the system (employees, geofences, escalation rules).

Priorities P1–P5

PriorityImpactNotification
P1No production impactStandard banner
P2Minor — attend soonStandard banner
P3Moderate — attend within the shiftBanner with action buttons
P4Significant — attend ASAPPersistent banner, DND bypass
P5Production standing — immediateFull-screen alarm, loud, lock-screen takeover

Operators pick the priority at creation. Use it honestly — a P5 escalates loudly and reaches managers fast.

Escalation

If no technician responds, the system escalates automatically across four configurable stages. Defaults:

StageDefault timeDefault stateReaches
Stage 15 minEnabledOn-site managers + foremen
Stage 210 minEnabledOn-site dept managers + workshop manager (urgent)
Stage 330 minDisabled by defaultConfigurable
Stage 460 minDisabled by defaultConfigurable

Stops the moment any technician taps "Assign to Me" or "I'm Busy". Admins tune timings and recipients in Settings → Escalation Rules.
