Priorities — Be Honest
P1 to P5
P1
Low impact — record it properly.
P2
Minor — still needs a real card.
P3
Needs attention this shift / soon.
P4
Serious — stronger notify behaviour.
P5
Production standing — full-screen style alarm for matching on-site techs. Not for “annoying”.
Fake P5s train the floor to ignore real ones.
Respond — Or Escalation Continues
Notifications
  • Assign Self — you take the job; status moves; escalation stops.
  • I'm Busy — after-hours path when available; still a real response.
  • Dismiss — escalation keeps running; your name is on the log.
Configurable escalation stages notify leads/managers if nobody accepts. The way out is simple: respond.

Ink · Waste · Security — Floor Truth
Modules

Ink

Capture production, meters, receive, DN photos. Managers cost on Pulse. Dual-running old system for financial confidence.

Waste

Schedule and collect with evidence. Weighbridge and cost are desk. Guard floor capture still depends on devices.

Security

Gate in/out, visitors, company cars. Guards never use Pulse security desk. Managers use Pulse for log and reports.

Fleet · Lurgi · Copper
Modules

Fleet

Reporters: problem + daily check. Mechanics: work records. Costing is Pulse only. Stores/hyster drivers still enrolling.

Lurgi

App paths built. Operational capture starts only when data entry is fully live and operators are rolled on.

Copper

Pre Press: sort / reuse / sell stages waste stock. Removal follows the waste path.

Pulse Overtime is test only — not live factory OT. Do not train it as production.

Everything Is Logged
Accountability
  • Who created the card, when, on which machine.
  • Who was notified and who assigned, dismissed, or went busy.
  • What was written on close — corrective action is permanent record.
  • Module capture (ink, waste, security, fleet) leaves the same kind of trail for the desk.
Good data today is the only way management and maintenance can trust the system tomorrow.
