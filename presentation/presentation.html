The Geofence — How We Know You're On Site
Automatic

What it does

An 800 m boundary around the site. The moment you cross in: you get "Arrived On-Site" and jobs start routing to you.

Cross out: "Left Site Area". Jobs stop routing to you.

All automatic — as long as Location is "Allow All the Time" and battery optimisation is off.

What it's NOT

✗ Not used to track movements off site
✗ Not used to log where you eat lunch
✗ Not used to "check on you"

It only ever checks: inside the boundary or outside the boundary. That's it.

Two reasons it exists: a technician at home shouldn't be alerted to a breakdown. And if you're off site, job alerts wait in your in-app inbox instead of pushing — the server won't fire a P5 alarm at someone who can't attend.
Anatomy of a Job Card
▶ LIVE DEMO

When operator creates

  • Department → Area → Machine → Part
  • Description of the fault
  • Priority 1–5
  • Type: Mechanical / Electrical / Mech-Elec

When technician works & closes

  • Notes — running observations, parts used, follow-ups
  • Photos — before, after, part numbers, damage
  • Complete (Corrective Action) — what you actually did to resolve the fault. Required at closure.
Dept → Area → Machine → Part is how the system later groups failures. Notes and Corrective Action are what teach it cause and cure. Skip a field, pick the wrong machine, or write nothing in Complete — that data is lost to us forever.

Why Accurate Notes Matter — 3 Payoffs
Preventive Maintenance

1. AI uses your notes as evidence

When another tech opens a job on the same machine + part, the system pulls your closure note and shows: "Last time this fault was fixed, here's exactly what was done."

"Fixed it" = they get nothing. Real detail = head start of hours.

2. Reoccurrence triggers PM

The system counts repeat faults per machine. Cross a threshold and the AI flags it to the workshop manager with a PM recommendation backed by your notes.

Your note becomes the reason the breakdown stops happening.

3. Fault-finding assistant learns from you

The in-app AI chatbot will be trained on closure notes. Tech on the floor asks "What was done last time the FX400 tripped?"

The answer is your closure note. Wrote nothing? They get nothing at 3am.

The deal: 60 extra seconds at closure. In return — fewer 2am callouts, fewer repeat breakdowns, the next tech gets your solution on their screen.
